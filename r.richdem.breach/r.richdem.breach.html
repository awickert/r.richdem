NAME

r.richdem.breach - Breach depressions in a DEM using RichDEM.

KEYWORDS

raster, hydrology, depression breaching

SYNOPSIS

r.richdem.breach
r.richdem.breach --help
r.richdem.breach input=name output=name [topology=string] [--overwrite] [--help] [--verbose] [--quiet]

Parameters:

input=name [required]
Input elevation raster
output=name [required]
Output depression-breached elevation raster
topology=string
Flow topology
Options: D8, D4
Default: D8

DESCRIPTION

r.richdem.breach removes topographic depressions from a digital elevation model (DEM) by carving a channel from each pit cell to the nearest outlet at the lowest possible elevation. Unlike depression filling, which raises pit cells to the pour-point elevation, breaching lowers the channel cells along the shortest path from the pit to a drainage outlet, thereby preserving the surrounding terrain.

Breaching is often preferable to filling when:

  • Depressions are small relative to surrounding terrain (narrow channels are more realistic than broad flat-bottomed basins).
  • Preserving the original elevation of non-channel cells is important for subsequent terrain analysis.
  • Filling would create large artificial flats that complicate flow-direction assignment.

Two flow topologies are supported:

  • D8 (default) — channel paths connect cells through any of the 8 neighbors.
  • D4 — channel paths connect cells through the 4 cardinal neighbors only.

NOTES

Breaching and filling are complementary approaches to hydrological conditioning. For many real-world landscapes both types of depressions exist: some depressions are artifacts that should be removed (filled or breached), while others are genuine features such as lakes and wetlands. To model water redistribution through real depressions rather than removing them, use r.richdem.dephier together with r.richdem.fsm.

After breaching, flat areas may remain at the carved channel elevations. r.richdem.resolveflats can impose a small drainage gradient on any remaining flat areas.

EXAMPLES

Breach depressions using D8 topology: 
r.richdem.breach input=dem output=dem_breached
Breach using D4 topology (cardinal neighbors only): 
r.richdem.breach input=dem output=dem_breached_d4 topology=D4
Compare filling versus breaching on the same DEM: 
r.richdem.fill input=dem output=dem_filled
r.richdem.breach input=dem output=dem_breached
r.mapcalc "diff = dem_filled - dem_breached"
